# ACIF Publisher Record Specification

**Version:** 0.1.0
**Status:** Draft
**Spec ID:** [ACIF-PUBLISHER]

---

## Abstract

This document defines the Level 2 layer of the Agent Content Interchange Format: the two-section published item record, the `publisher_section` and its `metadata_hash`, the frontmatter projection and reconciliation rules for publisher tooling, and the pack model — pack records, the pack-membership predicate, and the normative pack-inference algorithm.

## 1. Introduction

Level 1 of this document set defines canonical form per content type. This document defines what a *published record* of an item looks like: a single record with two sections whose boundary is the provenance boundary — what the publisher declared versus what a registry computed. It also defines packs, the grouping record by which a repository's items are published and discovered together.

The published record is carried in the item's sidecar ([ACIF-CORE] §6.1). Registry-computed content (`registry_section`) is specified in [ACIF-REGISTRY]; this document specifies the record structure, the publisher-declared section, and the rules binding tooling that projects canonical values back into source files.

## 2. Relationship to ACIF Core and the L1 Specifications

This document is the Level 2 (L2) companion to [ACIF-CORE] and depends on it normatively; it also depends normatively on the six L1 interchange specifications for each content type's extension-block definition. Conformance to this document requires conformance to [ACIF-CORE]. This document redefines no term from those documents.

This document is compatible with [ACIF-CORE] version 0.1.x. All documents are Draft maturity.

## 3. Terminology

Terms defined in [ACIF-CORE] §2 are used without redefinition. Additionally:

**published record** — the complete record of one item as published: `publisher_section` (when present) plus `registry_section`.

**`publisher_section`** — the faithful observation of the publisher's declared metadata (§5).

**`registry_section`** — the registry-computed section; schema in [ACIF-REGISTRY].

**declaration surface** — a carrier on which a publisher declares metadata: frontmatter on a source file, or a publisher-authored ACIF sidecar.

**frontmatter CI** — publisher-side tooling that projects canonical sidecar values into source-file frontmatter (§7).

**pack** — a publisher-scoped grouping of items with its own stable identity, carried as a `kind: pack` record (§8).

**declared pack / inferred pack** — a pack record authored by the publisher (`source_kind: declared`) / generated by a registry from the inference algorithm (`source_kind: inferred`).

## 4. Requirements Language

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "NOT RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as described in BCP 14 [RFC2119] [RFC8174] when, and only when, they appear in all capitals, as shown here.

## 5. The Two-Section Record

### 5.1 Structure is the provenance

Every published item record has at most two sections, and the section boundary is the only provenance marker:

- **`publisher_section`** — everything the publisher declared, observed faithfully. Never contains registry-computed values.
- **`registry_section`** — everything the registry computed. Always present on registry-emitted records; never sourced from publisher declarations ([ACIF-REGISTRY]).

There is no merge layer and there are no per-field provenance markers: a consumer that needs to know whether a value was declared or computed reads which section it is in.

### 5.2 `publisher_section` presence and content

`publisher_section` is present **if and only if** the publisher declared metadata on a declaration surface. Presence follows declaration, not content type:

- An item crawled from provider-native configuration with no frontmatter and no authored sidecar has no `publisher_section` (`registry_section.publisher_declared: false`).
- For **frontmatter-bearing** content types, a present `publisher_section` carries the declared envelope fields and the declared extension block ([ACIF-CORE] §7.8).
- For **sidecar-only** content types, a present `publisher_section` carries envelope-level fields only (`kind`, `id`, `display_name`, `version`, `description`, `license`, `pack_id`); the extension block's hash home is the `body_hash` preimage and it MUST NOT be duplicated into `publisher_section` ([ACIF-CORE] §7.8).

### 5.3 Faithful observation

`publisher_section` values are the publisher's declared values **as written**: no vocabulary translation, no default materialization, no normalization beyond the parse of the declaration surface itself. Registry-materialized defaults and vocabulary-translated forms are canonical-form content and MUST NOT be written into `publisher_section` ([ACIF-CORE] §7.8). *(Informative: this is what makes the section a provenance statement — its bytes answer "what did the publisher say," and `metadata_hash` (§6) attests exactly that. The canonical, translated, default-materialized form is what validators and predicates consume; the two views coexist by design.)*

### 5.4 Forbidden fields

The [ACIF-CORE] §5.2 forbidden version fields (`effective_version`, `derived_version`, `pack_inherited_version`, `resolved_version`) MUST NOT appear in either section of an item record. They MAY appear on a pack record only as the pack's own `version` semantics allow (§8.2): a pack's `version` is its own, never inherited into items.

### 5.5 Version discipline

Item `version` is literal-or-absent ([ACIF-CORE] §5.1): when declared it MUST be valid Semantic Versioning 2.0.0, and no implementation may synthesize, inherit, or derive a value when the publisher omits it. A pack's `version` lives on the pack record only and never propagates to member items. `body_hash` is the canonical change signal; `version` is advisory ordering and display.

## 6. `metadata_hash`

`metadata_hash` is REQUIRED on every record whose `publisher_section` is present, and MUST be absent otherwise.

**Preimage.** Let `P` be the canonical JSON serialization ([ACIF-CORE] §8.6, RFC 8785) of the `publisher_section` object with absent fields omitted. Arrays within `publisher_section` preserve declared order (declared metadata is observed, not canonicalized — §5.3). The preimage is `P` followed by a single line feed:

```
preimage             = UTF8(P) || 0x0A
metadata_hash.value  = lowercase-hex( SHA-256(preimage) )
metadata_hash.algorithm = sha256
```

**Scope (PATH α).** The preimage covers `publisher_section` bytes only. `metadata_hash` is therefore invariant under every registry-side change: pack-context changes, re-crawls, projection recomputes, and `registry_section` evolution never move it.

**Coverage consequences** ([ACIF-CORE] §7.8, normative in effect): for frontmatter-bearing types, re-targeting any declared extension-block value moves `metadata_hash` and not `body_hash`; for all types, a declared envelope edit (display name, description, license, `pack_id`) moves `metadata_hash` and not `body_hash`. The pair `(body_hash, metadata_hash)` covers the item's entire publisher-declared surface; `body_hash` remains the dispositive content change signal ([ACIF-CORE] §6.2). Consumer-surface exposure of `metadata_hash`, including its place in the change-signal tuple endpoint, is defined in [ACIF-REGISTRY].

## 7. Frontmatter Projection and Reconciliation

Data flows sidecar → frontmatter, never the reverse ([ACIF-CORE] §6.1). Tooling that projects canonical values into source files — the frontmatter CI — MUST read from the canonical sidecar and MUST follow these reconciliation rules per ACIF field:

| Condition in the source file | Default behavior | With explicit overwrite opt-in |
|---|---|---|
| Field absent | Add silently (non-destructive) | Add silently |
| Field present, value equal | Leave untouched | Leave untouched |
| Field present, value conflicts with the sidecar | **Block** and surface the conflict, reporting `acif.publisher.frontmatter_conflict` naming the field, both values, and the sidecar as the canonical source | Overwrite, and log the same identifier with the overwritten value |

Non-ACIF frontmatter fields always pass through untouched, under either setting. The overwrite behavior MUST be opt-in per run (an explicit tooling input), never a persistent default.

Because `publisher_section` observes declared values as written (§5.3), the conflict comparison is declared-value against canonical-sidecar value **for the same field**; a value that differs only because the sidecar carries a canonical vocabulary form of the same declaration is still a conflict under this rule — the projection exists to make the source file match the sidecar. Registries MUST NOT write to source files under any circumstances; reconciliation is publisher-side tooling only.

## 8. Pack Records

### 8.1 Schema

A pack is a `kind: pack` record:

```yaml
kind: pack
id: "f47ac10b-..."                    # UUIDv4 if declared; UUIDv5 (§9.4) if inferred
display_name: "superpowers"           # REQUIRED — mutable; never the identity
version: "5.1.0"                      # OPTIONAL — the pack's own version (§5.5)
description: "Skills and hooks for AI coding tools"
license:
  spdx: MIT
repository_url: "https://github.com/obra/superpowers"   # REQUIRED — canonical fetch URL

pack:
  source_kind: declared               # REQUIRED — declared | inferred
  canonical_address: "obra/superpowers"  # REQUIRED — <owner>/<display_name> (§9.5)
  inference_version: "v0.1"           # REQUIRED iff source_kind: inferred
```

**Identity.** A declared pack's `id` is a UUID version 4, generated once. An inferred pack's `id` is the UUID version 5 derivation of §9.4 — the one exception to the [ACIF-CORE] §5.1 UUIDv4 rule, noted there. `display_name` is mutable display text; renaming a pack changes no identity and breaks no `pack_id` reference.

### 8.2 Hashing

Pack records carry no canonical body: no `body_hash` and no `body_size_bytes` exist for packs. A **declared** pack record is publisher-declared metadata in full — its declared fields form its `publisher_section`, covered by `metadata_hash` per §6. An **inferred** pack record is registry-computed in full: no `publisher_section`, no `metadata_hash`, `registry_section` only.

### 8.3 Membership predicate

Membership is computed, never authored as a list:

```
item M ∈ pack P  iff
    M.publisher_section.pack_id == P.id
  OR
    (M.publisher_section.pack_id absent AND M.registry_section.inferred_pack_id == P.id)
```

Declared membership wins over inference when both exist and differ. The pack's `items` list is OUTPUT of this predicate, computed by the registry on every relevant change and carried in the pack record's `registry_section` ([ACIF-REGISTRY]); publishers do not author it.

Each item's `pack_resolution` state is one of `declared | inferred | unresolved`: `unresolved` means `publisher_section.pack_id` names a pack record that does not exist. Install tools MUST refuse items whose `pack_resolution` is `unresolved` unless the operator has explicitly opted in. Pack-less items — no declared `pack_id`, no inferred pack — are first-class: valid, publishable, installable.

## 9. Pack Inference Algorithm v0.1 (Normative)

A registry that emits `registry_section.inferred_pack_id` MUST implement this algorithm exactly; a registry that does not implement it exactly MUST omit `inferred_pack_id` entirely. Partially-conforming inference ("inferred somehow, as documented") is non-conformant.

### 9.1 Canonical source precedence

When a repository contains multiple pack-shaped manifests, the canonical source is the first present entry of:

1. `package.json` (top-level `name`)
2. `.claude-plugin/plugin.json` (top-level `name`)
3. `.cursor-plugin/plugin.json` (top-level `name`)
4. `.codex-plugin/plugin.json` (top-level `name`)
5. `gemini-extension.json` (top-level `name`)
6. Directory-adjacency fallback — the repository basename from `repository_url`

When multiple manifests exist with conflicting values, the registry adopts the precedence winner and SHOULD emit `acif.publisher.pack_source_conflict` naming the conflicting sources and values, surfaced to the publisher.

### 9.2 Canonical display name

`canonical_display_name = trim(source.name)` from the highest-precedence source: a single leading/trailing whitespace trim, no case folding, no internal whitespace collapsing. If the winning source's `name` is absent or empty, fall through to the next source.

### 9.3 Canonical repository URL

`canonical_repository_url` is the whole-URL-lowercased, https-normalized, trailing-slash-stripped, `.git`-suffix-stripped form of the repository fetch URL (e.g., `git@github.com:obra/superpowers.git` → `https://github.com/obra/superpowers`). "https-normalized" means: an scp-style `user@host:path` form is rewritten to `https://host/path`; any URL scheme (`ssh://`, `git://`, `http://`) is replaced with `https`, dropping a userinfo component if present.

This whole-URL lowercasing deliberately collapses casing variance into one repository identity and is **scoped to pack inference only** — it is a documented identity-collapse hazard, technically wrong for case-sensitive paths in general. The `source_uri` normalization ([ACIF-REGISTRY]) lowercases scheme and host only and preserves path case; the two pipelines serve different contracts and MUST NOT share an implementation.

### 9.4 `inferred_pack_id` derivation

```
inferred_pack_id = UUIDv5(
  namespace = ACIF_PACK_NAMESPACE,
  name      = canonical_repository_url + "\n" + canonical_display_name
)

ACIF_PACK_NAMESPACE = "93516344-00e5-419b-a230-6e8b1d02f87d"
```

The namespace constant is spec-pinned for inference algorithm v0.1 and is never reused by any other algorithm version; a future version MUST pin its own constant. Reference computation (TV-3): `UUIDv5(ACIF_PACK_NAMESPACE, "https://github.com/obra/superpowers\nsuperpowers")` = `d932cd6d-1c14-527d-b2e7-185c717b7a0d`.

### 9.5 Canonical address

`canonical_address = <owner>/<canonical_display_name>`, where `owner` is extracted from `canonical_repository_url` with the final path segment (the repository name) excluded throughout: for `github.com` and `bitbucket.org`, the single path segment before the repository name; for `gitlab.com`, the full group path (every segment before the repository name) joined with `/`; for `git.sr.ht`, the first path segment with a leading `~` stripped; for any other host, the first path segment.

### 9.6 Inference version

Every response containing `inferred_pack_id` MUST include `inference_version: "v0.1"` in the same `registry_section`. A consumer detecting inference-version drift between registries SHOULD surface the differing `inference_version` values to the operator; drift is a divergence signal, not an error.

## 10. Error Identifiers

| Identifier | Class | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| `acif.publisher.frontmatter_conflict` | diagnostic (CI block by default; logged on overwrite opt-in) | Declared frontmatter value conflicts with the canonical sidecar value for the same ACIF field (§7) |
| `acif.publisher.pack_source_conflict` | diagnostic (SHOULD-emit) | Multiple pack-shaped manifests carry conflicting values; the precedence winner was adopted (§9.1) |

The `pack_resolution: unresolved` install refusal (§8.3) is a state-vocabulary rule, not an error identifier; the state itself is the signal.

## 11. Security Considerations

**The section boundary is the trust boundary.** Everything in `publisher_section` is self-asserted ([ACIF-CORE] §11); everything in `registry_section` is asserted by the registry a consumer chose to trust. A record with no `publisher_section` has no publisher-attested metadata at all — `publisher_declared: false` is a signal consumers should surface, not smooth over.

**`metadata_hash` attests declaration, not truth.** It proves the declared bytes are unchanged since observation; it verifies nothing about their content. A licensing claim, a display name imitating a trusted publisher, and a `pack_id` claiming membership in a popular pack all hash identically whether honest or not. Pack-membership claims in particular are join requests: the §8.3 predicate honors any declared `pack_id`, so registries serving pack views should treat cross-publisher `pack_id` claims as an abuse surface (a stranger's item declaring membership in a popular pack inherits its discovery surface).

**Inferred identity is squattable in both directions.** The §9.4 derivation is deterministic from a repository URL and a manifest name: a fork or a re-created repository at the same URL yields the same inferred pack identity, and a typosquatted name yields a confusable one. Inference confers grouping, not endorsement; `source_kind: inferred` exists so consumers can distinguish computed grouping from publisher-claimed identity.

**Overwrite is destructive tooling.** The §7 overwrite opt-in writes into publisher source files. Block-by-default exists because a silent overwrite of a hand-authored frontmatter value is data loss with a signature indistinguishable from intent; the named diagnostic plus per-run opt-in keeps the destructive path audited.

**Version fields cannot carry sync semantics.** The §5.4/§5.5 rules exist because derived version fields were the historical path by which stale content masqueraded as current; `body_hash` is the only change signal, and the forbidden-field lint is enforceable.

## Appendix A — Conformance Test-Vector Families (Normative)

The vectors below, published in the `conformance/` directory, are normatively authoritative over prose. This appendix carries the record-layer and pack-layer vectors (TV-1 through TV-10 of the design record, plus the review-time additions TV-11 through TV-13):

- **TV-1** — `body_hash` stability across pack context: same skill body bytes under pack-less / pack-A / pack-B contexts → identical `body_hash.value`.
- **TV-2** — `metadata_hash` invariance (PATH α): same `publisher_section`, different inferred pack contexts → identical `metadata_hash.value`.
- **TV-3** — inference determinism: the §9.4 reference computation reproduces `d932cd6d-1c14-527d-b2e7-185c717b7a0d` byte-for-byte.
- **TV-4** — declared wins: `publisher_section.pack_id = X` with `registry_section.inferred_pack_id = Y` (X ≠ Y) → membership resolves via X.
- **TV-5** — unresolved pack: declared `pack_id` naming a nonexistent pack → `pack_resolution: unresolved`; install MUST refuse without operator opt-in.
- **TV-6** — forbidden fields: `effective_version` on an item record → conformance error.
- **TV-7** — literal version through pack context: item `version: 2.0.0` inside a pack declaring `5.1.0` → `publisher_section.version` is `2.0.0`, unchanged; no item-level field reflects `5.1.0`.
- **TV-8** — pack-less item is first-class: no `pack_id`, no `inferred_pack_id` → valid record, installable.
- **TV-9** — canonical `metadata_hash` bytes: for a published `publisher_section`, the §6 serialization matches the published byte string and the published reference hash.
- **TV-10** — pack rename preserves identity: `display_name` change → `id` unchanged; existing `pack_id` references resolve.
- **TV-L2 additions** *(promotion-time)*: (a) `publisher_section` presence follows declaration — a crawled no-frontmatter skill has no `publisher_section` and no `metadata_hash` (`publisher_declared: false`); the same skill republished with frontmatter gains both; (b) sidecar-only envelope-only rule — a hook with a publisher-authored sidecar declaring `display_name` + `pack_id` yields a `publisher_section` with envelope fields only, and its `metadata_hash` moves on a display-name edit while `body_hash` does not; (c) faithful observation — a declared provider-native tool spelling appears as written in `publisher_section` while the canonical form carries the translated name; `metadata_hash` is computed over the as-written form; (d) frontmatter CI matrix — absent-add / equal-noop / conflict-block with `acif.publisher.frontmatter_conflict` / overwrite-on-opt-in-with-log; (e) declared pack `metadata_hash` present; inferred pack record carries neither `publisher_section` nor `metadata_hash`; (f) *(review-time)* pack-source conflict — two pack-shaped manifests with conflicting values → the §9.1 precedence winner is adopted and `acif.publisher.pack_source_conflict` names the conflicting sources and values.

- **TV-11 through TV-13** *(review-time additions, [ACIF-CORE] §5/§7)*: envelope-field validation rejects (non-enum `kind`, non-UUIDv4 `id`, non-SemVer `version`, non-SPDX `license.spdx`); root-only sidecar exclusion in bytes (root `acif-sidecar.yaml` excluded, a same-named subdirectory file included); body-boundary rejects (`acif.body.symlink`, `acif.body.path_collision`, `acif.body.empty`).

Individual vector IDs are assigned in the conformance suite.
